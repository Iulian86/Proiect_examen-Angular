R.A.I.D. LOG

    RISKS

  • Are events that will adversely affect the project if they eventuate. Evaluate their importance based on the likelihood they will occur, along with the impact on the project if they do.
  • Ask: What events might occur they will have a negative impact?
  • Actions: Implement risk mitigation strategies based on the critically of each risk.

    • ASSUMPTIONS

  • Are aspects of the project that you assume will be in place to help the project run but can not be guaranteed. If they are proved wrong, there will be an impact on the project.
  • Ask: What exists, or do we presume to be true, that will be an impact on the project?
  • Actions: Reassess assumptions at regular intervals to ensure they are still valid.

    • ISSUES

  • Are events that have an adverse impact on the project. They are risks that have eventuated, and you must manage ASAP to keep the project on track.
  • Ask: What must we deal with to make the project run to plan?
  • Actions: Contain or remove the issues.

    • DEPENDENCIES

  • Are activities which need to start or be completed so the project can progress and succeed. Dependencies may rely on internal or external events, suppliers, or partners.
  • Ask: Who or what are we dependent on and who depends on us?
  • Actions: Monitor and manage dependencies.
R.A.I.D. LOG

    Contains all the working chapters with the following details:

  • ITEM DESCRIPTION: Description of every item, chapter, subchapter and works list. This same structure will be later used in site reports.
  • MEASUREMENT UNIT: Measurement units for every type of work.
  • QUANTITY: Quantities for every type of work.
  • COST/MEASUREMENT UNIT: Costs per measurement unit for every type of work.
  • TOTAL COST: Total cost for every type of work.

SITE REPORTS
  • Are standardised reports for every type of the construction works made every day, including quantities done in that specific day
  • It follows the same list of works first implemented into the bill of quantities list.
  • It helps track the working progress for every article, by every day.
  • It helps optimizing and schedule the manpower for every work.
  • It is structured on the basis of local standardised working forms.
SITE REPORTS
  • Are specific reports containing general information, made once a week, in order to generally inform the owner about overall progress of the project.
  • It can contain information regarding time schedule, problems that needs to be solved, design issues and proposals, etc.
  • It's not a standardized report, it is more like a general informed report.
